Welcome to Karambi Youth Initiative

Dignity For Every Youth and Vulnerable Youth in Kasese District.

Our Vision

To have a free community with raised voices, freedom of expression, and self-sustainability through vocational activities to secure the future of the members of the community.

Our Mission

To improve the livelihoods of the youth and stakeholders, especially school dropouts, vulnerable youth, orphans, and widows through vocational training, skill development, and economic empowerment.

Our Goal

Transformation of the youth (school dropouts) and vulnerable groups through engagement in vocational activities, environmental and water conservation, and sensitization.

Our Organizational Objectives

  • Increase access to education for the youth and improve their literacy level.
  • Fight stigma against youth in the community.
  • Create awareness of youth potential to change negative community attitudes and raise self-esteem.
  • Facilitate processes that improve the economic status of youth, caretakers, and stakeholders.

Our Organizational Activities

  • Poverty eradication and encouraging income generation through vocational activities.
  • Promoting youth talents through music production.
  • Fighting for youth labor rights.
  • Raising awareness about the usefulness of youth in communities.
  • Sensitizing the masses on gender issues.
  • Providing guidance and counseling for youth and vulnerable groups.
  • Sensitizing on climate and environmental issues.
  • Promoting clean water conservation and encouraging water harvesting practices.

Our Organizational Core Values

  • Mutual trust
  • Transparency
  • Active participation
  • Valuing commitments by youth and marginalized groups
  • Love and care for the youth

Our Target Groups

  • Vulnerable youth (without discrimination)
  • Parents, guardians, and all stakeholders
  • School dropouts (Boys and Girls)
  • All members of the community

Demographics We Focus On

  • Adolescent Girls
  • Young Women
  • Young Men
  • Adolescent Boys
  • Youth with Disabilities
  • Youth from Indigenous Populations
